# Predictions — the Monday flagship ("my week, called from Threadverse")

A weekly recurring format: you read the *early signals* your Threadverse feed surfaced over the weekend and
**call what breaks big this week** — then next Monday you score yourself. This makes the product the ENGINE of
the content (not an afterthought): the whole premise is "I can predict this *because* my feed catches signal
before the timeline." Forward-looking = shareable + comment-bait ("you're wrong about #3").

**Why this converts without being an ad:** the value IS the product's job. You're demonstrating the feed working,
live, by using it to see around corners. Named naturally, shown on screen (the feed is the visual), never pitched.

**Format:** ~45-70s (longer than a news short — predictions earn playtime). Recurring open + numbered calls +
a confidence tag + a "screenshot this" close that drives saves/returns. Film the feed (pro session, sanitized).

**Cadence:** post Monday AM. Next Monday, open by grading last week's calls (accountability = trust + a reason
to come back). Keep a running scoreboard in the caption/pinned comment.

## Reusable template (swap the 5 calls each week)

```
COLD OPEN   — "Every Monday I call what breaks, from my feed's weekend signals. Last week: X/5. Screenshot this."
CALL 1-5    — each = [the signal the feed caught] → [the prediction] → [confidence]. Mix 3 safe + 1 spicy + 1 arguable.
             The spicy/arguable ones drive comments; the safe ones build the track record that earns trust.
CLOSE       — "All from one feed reading 5,000 posts. Save it, back Monday, we keep score."
```
**Rules**
- Every call must trace to a REAL signal in the feed (show it on screen). No vibes-only predictions — the
  product's credibility is the whole format; a call with no visible source breaks the premise.
- Confidence tags are honest: label the reach as a reach. Being *right about being unsure* also builds trust.
- The arguable call (#5 here) is deliberate comment-bait — a specific, falsifiable claim people will defend or dunk.
- **Accountability is the moat:** grade last week openly. "I was wrong about X" is the most trust-building thing
  you can say, and it's the reason the audience returns every Monday.
- Product named ≤ twice, always as "my feed / one feed" doing its job (reading 5,000 → surfacing the signal).
  Passes the delete test only loosely here (the feed IS the method) — that's fine; this is the ONE format where
  the product is allowed to be central, because the value delivered is literally the product working.
- Fact-check every number before filming (the % odds, the raises, the dates move fast).
