# The ONE Avatar — every script is for this exact person

The algorithm builds a fit-score from content signals. If scripts point at different people, the
sample data comes back mixed and reach dies. So: **one avatar, every script.** Uniqueness comes from
different STORIES for this person — never a different person.

## Who they are
**"The person drowning in information who wants to stay sharp — without the noise."**
- Tech-native, 22–38. Dev / researcher / founder / operator / serious student.
- Reads Hacker News, follows AI + tech closely, dips into gaming/science/crypto **as an informed observer** (not a degen, not a wellness-seeker).
- Their core pain: **too much to keep up with, most of it garbage, and they hate feeling behind.**
- Their self-image: smart, curious, allergic to hype, slightly superior about not falling for slop.
- What they want: to be *early* and *informed* while spending *less* time in the feed.

## The signals every script MUST send (so the algorithm files it right)
- Language of **information / signal / staying current / reading / knowing** — not "wellness", "planet", "manifest", "touch grass", "degen", "conspiracy".
- Assume competence: they build/ship/research. Talk to a peer, not a patient.
- Curiosity + a dry, confident, faintly contrarian tone. Never hypey, never self-helpy.
- Lanes = flavor INSIDE their interests: AI (home turf), science ("wait, what" findings), gaming (as a smart hobby), crypto (as tech/mechanism-design, not trading), philosophy (as "how to think" for sharp people).

## Anti-signals — if a script smells like these, it's the WRONG avatar
- ❌ Eco / climate / "save the planet" framing → files under environmentalist
- ❌ "Touch grass" / screen-time-guilt / self-help → files under wellness/productivity-guru
- ❌ "Not your keys" / DAO / trading-degen → files under crypto-native
- ❌ "The algorithm is a conspiracy" / anti-tech → files under anti-tech/doomer
- ❌ Generic "your feed is toxic" consumer framing → files under normie

## The self-check (run on every script before it ships)
> "If the algorithm only saw this script, would it show it to a curious dev/researcher who reads HN
> and wants to stay ahead? Or to someone else?" — If *someone else*, reframe or kill it.

## How the reframe works (same idea, right avatar)
- Env impact → **"good systems design"**: "a million people each sifting the same noise is just bad engineering. One system reads it once." (efficiency, not planet)
- Reclaimed time → **"deep work / focus"**: "you can't ship if you're context-switching into a feed every 20 min." (output, not touch-grass)
- Own your feed → **"you configure it, like a tool"**: plain-English config, not sovereignty/crypto.
- Crypto stories → **"signal for people who track crypto as tech"**: mechanism, exploits-as-engineering, not trading calls.
