# Addictive Storytelling — the framework every script must run

Synthesized from the 3 short-form videos (algorithm + hooks + addictive storytelling).
Scripts are ABOUT THREADVERSE (marketed so it doesn't feel like marketing). The lane/discovery
is texture; **the product is the story.**

## Why stories hold: dopamine = the PREDICTION chemical (not pleasure)
The brain fires dopamine while ANTICIPATING an outcome, not receiving it. Uncertainty = the drug.
A vending machine (predictable) is boring; a slot machine (uncertain) is addictive. Stories that
keep an *unresolved prediction* running are chemically impossible to leave.

## The 4-step addiction loop (run on REPEAT, no gaps)
1. **STAKES** — turn the prediction machine ON. Needs 3 things:
   - a **character to root for** (can be "you", the viewer, or "me")
   - **something at risk** (a consequence if it goes wrong)
   - **urgency** (a ticking clock — "by the end of the week" > "eventually")
   - Stakes ≠ life/death. Just *personally relevant to the tech-native viewer* (missing the news, drowning in noise, wasted hours, fried attention).
2. **BIG QUESTION** — give *enough specifics* to make them PREDICT (not vague "you won't believe").
   Load it EARLY so the dopamine drips longer before the answer.
3. **HEAD FAKE** — a reveal that BREAKS their prediction but is **logical in hindsight** (the clues
   were there). Great head fake = "never saw it coming, but instantly makes sense." The product IS
   the head fake: *"I thought I was just lucky / naturally informed… no — an AI read 5,000 posts and kept 50."*
4. **REHOOK** — close one loop and open a new one IN THE SAME BREATH. Zero gap.
   Connective phrases: *"which would've been great, except…"*, *"but here's the problem…"*,
   *"and that's when I realized…"*, *"which is exactly why…"*

## Fused with HOOKS (first 1-2s) + ALGORITHM (narrow lane)
- **Hook** = STAKES stated with topic clarity + on-target curiosity, in line 1. Kill delay/confusion/
  irrelevance ("you/your")/disinterest (contrast A-vs-B).
- **Algorithm** = keep each script in ONE lane's flavor so the sample stays clean. Product is constant;
  lane is the seasoning.

## The Threadverse story engine (the reusable spine)
> **STAKES:** you (the viewer) are drowning in noise / missing what matters / your attention is fried.
> **BIG QUESTION:** how does [someone] always catch the news first / stay sane / read less but know more?
> **HEAD FAKE:** it's not willpower or luck — one feed, an AI reading 5,000 posts, keeping the 50 that matter.
> **REHOOK:** …and the wild part is [second angle: the env cost / the rejected pile / what it caught] →

## Script anatomy (20-40s, talking-to-camera OR VO-over-visuals)
```
HOOK (0-2s)        one line: stakes + clarity + curiosity
STAKES (2-6s)      character + risk + ticking clock
BIG QUESTION       the specific thing to predict
[beat / VISUAL]    the drip — build anticipation
HEAD FAKE          the product reveal that breaks the prediction, logical in hindsight
REHOOK             close + open a new loop in one breath
CTA (last 2s)      soft, earned: "threadverse.ai" / "link in bio" — never salesy
```
Each script includes `[VISUAL: …]` cues per beat so visuals can be generated per line.

## Rules
- NEVER lead with "Threadverse is an app that…". Lead with the viewer's pain or a curiosity gap.
- The product is revealed as the HEAD FAKE (the satisfying answer), not announced up front.
- One unresolved question always running — no dead air, no "anyway".
- Vary the ANGLE every script (see angles.md) so 50 never feel same-y.
- End earned, not begged. The CTA is the natural next step, not a pitch.

## Delivery & production hacks (from the psychology playbook — apply when filming/VO)
- **Record one line at a time.** Talking in paragraphs makes you trail off and lose energy at sentence-ends.
  Deliver each line at full energy, chop together in the edit. Single biggest energy lever. (Energy is
  contagious — mirror neurons; bring delivery up ~50% vs. how you'd read it flat.)
- **Trance rhythm — want a JAGGED right margin.** Vary sentence length: short, then long, then compound.
  If every sentence is the same length the script reads monotone → churn. Glance down the script; if the
  line-ends form a straight edge, break it up.
- **Say the technical thing twice** — once precisely, once plain + a metaphor. Raises comprehension →
  likeability (they feel smart). (This is also the fix for misfires like "cache vs filter": say "filtering,
  done by hand" then the plain-English restatement.)
- **Lead with the single best item first** (the "hunt"): front-load the most valuable/surprising thing to
  validate staying, then tease more buried later ("stay to the end — one's about to be worth a trillion").
