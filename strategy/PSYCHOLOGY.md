# The 6-Checkpoint Viewer Journey (distilled from the Content Psychology Playbook)

The hook/story/algorithm docs tell you *what* makes a good video. This tells you the **order the viewer's
brain moves through** — six gates, each a place they bounce if you miss it. Our 6-gate SCRIPT-CHECKLIST is
about *the script passing the algorithm*; this is about *the viewer not leaving*. Use it to sequence a script.

Checkpoints 1–3 all happen **inside the hook** (first 10–30s). 4 is ambient. 5–6 run through the body/close.

## Checkpoint 0 — the 3-bucket sort (happens BEFORE anything else, in the first ~1s)
The instant a viewer lands, their brain files the content into one of three buckets:
```
POTENTIAL SOLUTION  → they lock in a completely different way (this is the only bucket that converts)
ENTERTAINMENT       → "junk-food view" — they watch, forget it in 3 videos, never act
IRRELEVANT          → gone
```
**The trap that ruins conversion is landing in ENTERTAINMENT while thinking you're in SOLUTION.** A post that
just restates news everyone already knows gets filed as entertainment at best — a pleasant scroll, zero trust,
zero action. To land in *solution*, the content must signal **"this is new / useful / for you"** — i.e. it has
to tell them something **non-obvious** they can actually use, not confirm what they already believe.

**This is the deepest test of all our content: is it genuinely NEW to the avatar, or are we regurgitating?**
- Trust accrues *only* when you say something non-obvious + useful they haven't heard (PRINCIPLES #1). Restating
  a headline they saw this morning accrues **nothing** — it's obvious, so it files as entertainment/irrelevant.
- The avatar is a sharp HN-reading dev. Their bar for "non-obvious" is HIGH. "AI is moving fast" / "your feed is
  noisy" / "big tech bad" = things they already believe = instant entertainment-bucket = throttled + no convert.
- **The fix is a real TAKE** (a contrarian angle, a mechanism they didn't see, a reframe) — which is exactly why
  the contrarian-stance rework matters. A hot take they'd argue with lands in *solution* ("wait, is he right?").
- **Confusion also dumps you into IRRELEVANT** — if they can't parse it in 1–2 lines, they don't bucket it as a
  solution, they bounce. Clarity (Gate 1) is what keeps a genuinely-new idea from being mis-filed.

Run this as a pre-check on every script/post: *"Would this HN dev think 'huh, I hadn't thought of that' — or
'yeah, I know'?"* If it's "I know," it's regurgitation → rewrite the take until it's new.

```
1. PAINPOINT      → make them feel the pain you solve       (or they never opt in)
2. TRUST          → pass the BS detector in 10–30s          (or they don't believe you)
3. PLAN           → show you have a different, ordered plan  (or they churn)
4. LIKEABILITY    → make them WANT you to be the one        (disarms inhibition)
5. ATTENTION      → open loops, rehook, rhythm              (hold them to the end)
6. ACTION         → close the loop → one clear next step    (the whole point)
```

## 1 — Painpoint acceptance
The brain is a problem-solving machine; hand it a problem and it can't not engage. Four ways to plant it:
- **One standard deviation away.** Don't name the deep desire directly ("make money") — it trips the BS
  detector. Name the *proxy* pain they accept ("you're not staying on top of AI fast enough") and let them
  make the leap. **Our avatar's accepted pain = "you're drowning / falling behind / your feed is noise,"** NOT
  "you want to be smarter" (too on-the-nose).
- **Twist the knife.** Name the frustration bluntly. FOMO/jealousy work ("the stuff everyone's already talking about while you missed it").
- **Audience of one.** Say **"you,"** never "I." Make them the main character.
- **Painpoint→solution gap.** Make them feel the *distance* between where they are (buried) and where they want to be (sharp, ahead).

## 2 — Sprint to trust (the Trust Ladder)
You have 10–30s to pass the BS detector. Ways to earn trust, **strongest → weakest**:
1. **Proof you solved this for someone like them** (testimonial they see themselves in) — strongest.
2. Proof you did it **for yourself** (personal result).
3. **Massive audience / a cosign** from someone they trust.
4. You've **been in their shoes** and aren't anymore (underwent the transformation).
5. **Common ground** ("I've been exactly where you are").
6. **Studio/production quality** — weakest alone, but real. A polished setup on a small account *inverts*
   ("no proof yet but great setup → they root for you"). This is why suits and clean sets exist.

Rule: **any ONE of the top three ≈ enough. If you have none, stack multiple of the bottom three.**
*Bigger ask → more trust needed. Deeper pain → less trust needed.*

**For us (a brand with no face/testimonials yet):** we buy trust with rungs 5–6 — clean visuals, and *the
value itself being real* (demonstration-of-ability: the finding/fact is genuinely non-obvious and correct).
Every accurate, non-obvious script IS a trust deposit. Over-claiming or a wrong technical analogy is a
withdrawal (cf. the "cache vs filter" miss). This is why fact-checking the news briefs matters beyond honesty.

## 3 — Plan of attack
Once they trust you, they want to know you have a *different* plan.
- **Point of difference** = contrast: they expect Y, you say Z, and Z is better. (Same engine as the hook.)
- **Ordered list.** Structure makes them feel smart; feeling smart → they credit you → more trust. "3 things,"
  "here's what the feed caught this week." Tee up the structure *before* the tactics or they feel lost.

## 4 — Likeability
People disarm for people they like. Three levers:
- **Energy** — mirror neurons: bring delivery up ~50%. **Best hack: record one line at a time** (talking in
  paragraphs makes you trail off and lose energy at sentence-ends). Chop together after.
- **Simplification** — simple words, short sentences; **say the technical thing twice** — once jargon, once
  plain + a metaphor. Making them feel dumb kills likeability.
- **Authenticity** — don't play a character; consistent voice/look. People sense it and root for you.

## 5 — Harness attention (hold them through the body)
- **Create a hunt.** Lead with the single most valuable thing FIRST (dopamine hit that validates staying),
  then tease more buried later. Open a long loop while closing small ones ("stay to the end, one's about to
  be worth a trillion" — exactly the news-brief move).
- **Rehook** at every seam where a loop closes — reignite the pain→solution gap with an "and/but flip":
  "that point was good, but if you miss *this* it won't matter."
- **Trance rhythm.** Vary sentence length — short, then long, then compound. If your script's right margin is
  a straight line (all sentences same length) it reads monotone → churn. **Want a jagged margin.**

## 6 — Drive action (close)
- **Close the loop.** Signal the pain→solution gap is now closed AND re-signal it's not *fully* solved on one
  watch → they act to finish closing it. (For us the action is: try the feed free / follow — NOT buy. See FUNNEL.)
- **Unexpected surprise.** Over-deliver vs expectation; a "12/10" video earns the action.
- **The recap.** Tell them what you'll do, do it, remind them at the end — the final nail on the loop.

## How this maps to our constraints
- Our **action is a free signup / follow**, never an in-video Pro pitch (Gate 4 + FUNNEL). Checkpoint 6 is soft.
- Our **trust** comes from rungs 5–6 (clean craft + genuinely-correct value), since we have no testimonials.
- Checkpoints 1→3 must all land inside the hook — that's *why* the hook takes the most work.
- One-line-at-a-time recording + jagged sentence rhythm are the two delivery hacks worth adopting immediately.
