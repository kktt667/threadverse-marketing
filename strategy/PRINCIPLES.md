# Operating Principles (the "9 lies" — content myths, corrected for our account)

Strategic corrections from the personal-branding playbook. These sit *above* the tactical gates: they're the
worldview the whole channel runs on. Each = the lie, the truth, and what it means for Threadverse.

## 1 — There's no magic content formula → "time under trust"
The only universal metric: **minutes watched × trust accrued.** Trust accrues when you tell people something
**non-obvious AND tactically useful** they haven't heard. Obvious = no trust. Interesting-but-useless = no trust.
→ **For us:** every post must clear *non-obvious + useful*. A restated headline earns nothing. The feed's whole
value prop ("catches what you'd have missed") only lands if what we surface is genuinely early/non-obvious.

## 2 — "You are the niche" is a lie → the TOPIC is the niche
Only mega-fandoms transcend their topic. For everyone else, the algorithm punishes topic-hopping. Don't
document-your-life / build-in-public / flex hobbies. Personality is the **icing**; the **cake** is the topic +
your unique take. → **For us:** the cake is our 5 lanes (AI/science/gaming/crypto/philosophy) for the ONE avatar.
"Threadverse the brand" is not the niche yet — *the curated insight* is. Keep lanes tight (cf. NICHE.md, Gate 0).

## 3 — Bad hooks aren't why videos flop → weak TOPIC or weak TAKE is
If the topic isn't something people care about, or your take isn't new/contrarian, no hook saves it. Hook,
editing, captions are all *downstream* of topic + take. The exercise: *"what do I believe about this that 99%
haven't heard or would disagree with?"* = your contrarian take. → **For us:** pick validated topics (cult-loved
models/studios/thinkers) and always carry a real angle. The news brief's spice ("too dangerous to use, safe to
sell the stock?") is a *take*, not decoration — that's what makes it defensible vs. a plain news readout.

## 4 — "Data kills creativity" is a lie → data-enabled creativity
Don't AI-print content (trends to average) and don't pure-guess (starving artist). Use data to **aim** — study
what already works in your niche — then be creative on the last mile (twist/remix/reinvent). → **For us:** we
already study winning hooks and score cards; keep mining what performs per lane, then remix, never copy.

## 5 — "More views = more money" is a lie → audience-offer alignment
The right viewer can be worth 1,000× a random one. TAM/virality matters less than **offer↔audience↔content
alignment.** Chasing big off-niche viral hits is worse than depth. → **For us:** a viral hit to the *wrong* tribe
(eco/crypto-degen/normie) actively hurts — it poisons the sample group (Gate 0) AND those users never convert.
Depth in the avatar > breadth. One on-avatar signup >> a hundred off-avatar views.

## 6 — "Create for everyone in your niche" is a lie → audience of one
Write for a single real person so specific it'd be impossible for them not to share it. → **For us:** write to
one HN-reading dev/founder drowning in tabs. If it's a bullseye for him, it's a bullseye for his tribe.

## 7 — Quantity-vs-quality is a false binary → three phases
- **Phase 1 (skill acquisition, ~20–30 vids):** quantity. Make one, post one, *learn*, repeat — NOT batch (batching
  delays learning to video 21). Push through "the suck"; everyone who quit, quit here.
- **Phase 2 (~20–30 vids):** install a **quality floor** — best you could do *given current skill* (idea, research,
  delivery, packaging — not fancy visuals). ~3–5 posts/week, never dip below the floor.
- **Phase 3 (forever):** hold a ~9/10 floor, slowly ramp quantity back up.
→ **For us:** we're early (725 views, ~3 followers = Phase 1). **Post often, learn fast, one-at-a-time not batched.**
Don't over-polish while learning; don't chase 10/10 yet. Get reps, read the data, hold a rising floor.

## 8 — "Be everywhere all the time" is a lie → one home platform first
Platforms differ (LinkedIn older/pro/behind-trends; YouTube Shorts younger/pop-culture; IG+TikTok between).
Pick ONE home platform, dominate it, *then* earn the right to expand. The "modern content stack" = short-form
home + long-form (YouTube) + email, but you don't start there. → **For us (locked to our real data):**
- **TikTok = HOME.** ~725 views/post vs. ~40 on IG — TT is where both the reach *and* the feedback signal are.
  Optimize hooks/edits to what TikTok rewards; judge "is it working?" by **TikTok's** numbers.
- **IG Reels = consistency crosspost only.** IG reach stays low *by design* until the account ages into trust,
  so we crosspost to bank consistency + reputation, NOT to optimize. Don't get discouraged by the 40.
  **Switch trigger:** when IG crossposts start clearing **~150–200 organically on their own**, the algorithm has
  begun trusting us — *then* it's worth investing in IG-native optimization. Until then, TT gets the brain, IG gets the file.
- **YouTube = HOLD** (round two, once short-form is humming — see INDEX.md).
- **Cross-posting rule:** export **clean, no TikTok watermark**, then upload natively to IG (a competitor watermark
  is the one thing that genuinely suppresses IG reach). Reposting the same content is otherwise NOT penalized —
  the platforms don't see each other. Tune the caption per platform; drop `#Threadverse`-type brand tags on video (Gate 4).
- The *static* library still auto-posts Bluesky+Mastodon — that's separate from the short-form video home.

## 9 — "Jump on trends" is a lie → start trends, don't chase them
Copying trending hooks/audio/formats makes you a derivative clone — views without trust, no brand lift. Zag when
others zig; use data to see what's working so you can remix *away* from it and own a lane. → **For us:** our edge
is *native curation-driven insight*, not trend-hopping. Use trends only as raw material to remix into our angle.

**Minutes × trust, on a narrow topic, for one avatar, with a real take, learned through reps.** Everything in
