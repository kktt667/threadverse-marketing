# The Funnel — how free reach converts to paid Pro (without breaking the gates)

The mistake to avoid: pitching Pro in the short-form videos. That fails Gate 4 (reads as an ad →
algorithm throttles it) AND is bad funnel logic (a cold viewer can't convert to paid). Conversion is
a RELAY across stages, each doing one job.

## The relay
```
1. SHORT-FORM (TikTok/IG)   → AWARENESS + "try it free"     ← the 50 scripts. Free-first. Never pitch Pro.
2. BIO + PINNED COMMENT     → the CLICK → free signup         ← soft, one line. Pro only hinted, if at all.
3. THE PRODUCT (free tier)  → free→Pro conversion             ← THE MONEY STEP. The natural wall does it.
4. YOUTUBE + warm content   → the deeper "why Pro" case       ← warm audience; a real Pro case is allowed here.
```
Each stage only has to move someone to the NEXT stage. Short-form's job is *free signup*, not *paid*.

## The money step: the natural wall (our chosen lever)
Free plan = Hacker News + Bluesky + Mastodon · 3 feeds · 10 refreshes/day · NO X, NO YouTube · occasional sponsored.
Pro (£25/mo) = all platforms (adds **X + YouTube**) · unlimited feeds · 200 refreshes/day · **zero sponsored**.

**Why this converts on its own:** a hooked free user who wants X or YouTube in their feed hits the wall
having ALREADY felt the value. That's the highest-intent moment — they upgrade because they want more of
a thing they already love, not because a video told them to. Our whole job upstream is to get quality
free signups; the product closes them.

**Implication for the videos:** make the FREE experience look irresistible. Don't sell Pro. The scripts
already do this (they show the core magic: plain-English filtering, the reject pile, the feed that ends).

## Where Pro is allowed to appear (warm points only)
- **Pinned comment / bio:** one soft, optional line — see below. Never a hard "upgrade now."
- **YouTube** ("How I use Threadverse"): warm viewers; you can honestly show X/YouTube feeds = Pro, and say so.
- **Build-in-public / email / retargeting:** warmest; a direct Pro case is fine here.
- **The product's own UI:** the upgrade prompt at the wall. (Not our job, but it's the real closer.)

## Pinned comment (seeds free signup; Pro only hinted)
Default (free-first, no Pro mention):
> been getting asked what feed this is — it's threadverse.ai. describe what you want in plain english,
> it reads ~5,000 posts and keeps the ~50 worth it. free to start.

Optional Pro-hint variant (use sparingly, e.g. on platform-breadth videos):
> it's threadverse.ai — free covers Hacker News, Bluesky & Mastodon. (X and YouTube are on Pro.)

## Bio (every platform)
> AI reads 5,000 posts/day so you see the 50 worth your time. one feed, zero noise. free → threadverse.ai

Bio can carry the link + a whisper of the tiers. The VIDEO never does.

## The soft Pro moments already in the 50 (kept native, not pitches)
These stay value-first (pass the gates) but naturally point at Pro capabilities:
- **"All five platforms, one feed"** — the cross-platform value IS the Pro tier. Curious viewer signs up free → discovers X/YouTube are Pro.
- **Script 48 "the honest math"** — £25 < ChatGPT + X Premium, framed as a spending-audit insight (product ambient).
- **"Zero sponsored / the feed that's pure signal"** — Pro removes sponsored; framed as an anti-ad value hook.
Do NOT convert these into "upgrade" CTAs. Their power is that they're not.

## The one rule
Short-form = get them in free. The product + warm channels = get them to pay. Never collapse those two,
or you lose the reach that fills the funnel in the first place.
