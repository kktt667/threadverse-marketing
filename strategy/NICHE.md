# Threadverse — Niche & Avatar (Bluesky · Mastodon · X)

Research-backed. This is the discipline that makes the algorithm push us (see HOOKS.md for why:
narrow topic + narrow avatar, repeated, = clean sample data = more reach).

## The avatar (SAME across all 3 text platforms — do not mix)
Tech-native, **18–34, ~62% male**, curious, early-career devs / researchers / founders / students.
They already believe **"my feed is broken and noisy."** Threadverse solves their exact pain.

**Why this avatar (data):**
- **Bluesky** — 43.5M users, 56% under 35, the *de facto* home for academics/scientists (39k+ influential science accounts, +75% growth) with strong **philosophy / ethics / cognitive-science** communities. Discovery = custom feeds, not algorithm. → hashtags + niche clustering matter.
- **Mastodon** — ~785k MAU but *exceptional* engagement in tech niches; the people who built open-source (devs/researchers/journalists), privacy-conscious, anti-enshittification. Threadverse's "own your feed, no algorithm, no ads" pitch is native here.
- **X** — same tech/AI crowd at bigger scale (manual posting).

## The 5 lanes (narrow — only post these; always tie back to Threadverse)
1. **AI** — model releases, breakthroughs, the discourse/drama, capability jumps
2. **Science / Tech** — research, discoveries, "wait, really?" findings *(the trend you found)*
3. **Gaming** — releases, dev culture, indie gems, the craft
4. **Philosophy** — modern & relevant; ethics, cognition, "how to think" *(huge on Bluesky)*
5. **Crypto** — kept: it's core tech culture for this crowd (exploits, mechanism design, discourse)

**CUT from these 3 platforms** (they confuse the algorithm for this avatar): wildlife, true crime,
wholesome, internet drama (unless tech drama), sports, generic viral, travel, gym.
(Those can live on other channels later, but never in the tech-avatar feeds.)

## The through-line (every post)
The niche content is the **discovery layer** (the value/hook). Threadverse is the **payoff** (how they
got it). Pattern: *"[surprising AI/science/gaming/philosophy/crypto thing]. …found in my Threadverse feed / this is the stuff my feed keeps."*
Never a naked ad. Always: real curated post → framed as proof the product works.

## Per-platform lane MIX (concrete targets — enforced in build-queue.js)
Same avatar everywhere; what changes is *which lane leads*. This is per-platform *audience matching*, NOT a
new tribe. Each platform must stay consistent **to itself** so its fingerprint compounds. Observed (2026-07):
Bluesky went ~60% gaming on its own and **popped**; Mastodon was a scattered 37/30/18/9/7 and stayed **flat**
(no dominant signal = confused fingerprint). So:

### Bluesky — RIDE + BRIDGE gaming (it's winning; steer it toward the niche)
Target mix: **gaming ~60% · ai ~20% · science ~10% · philosophy ~10%**. Gaming is the proven fingerprint — feed it.
- **BRIDGE angle:** frame gaming *tech-ward* so it doubles as AI/tech content and pulls the audience toward the
  money niche: game engines (UE5/nanite), **AI in games** (NPCs, procedural, upscaling), GPU/hardware, **studio
  economics as market signal** (layoffs, acquisitions), dev-culture/craft. Avoid pure fandom/console-war rage.
- Why: rides the momentum AND converts a gaming crowd into a tech-curious one that can want Threadverse.
- 3–6 hashtags, broad+niche mix. "Own your feed" framing lands hard.

### Mastodon — AI/TECH-NATIVE lead (fix the scatter; match FOSS/privacy culture)
Target mix: **ai ~40% · science ~30% · philosophy ~20% · (crypto/gaming ≤10% combined)**. Trim crypto+gaming
hard — they're what scattered the fingerprint. AI+science is both the platform's native audience (devs who
built open-source, researchers) AND our conversion niche. Substance-first, no hype. CamelCase hashtags.
- Why Mastodon was flat: no lead lane. One clean AI/science signal, posted consistently, is what compounds here.

### X (manual) — AI + science + crypto hot-takes. Link-free captions; link in bio/pinned.

## The one rule
Even a viral hit in the WRONG niche hurts the next several posts (confuses the fit-score). Stay in **that
platform's** lane mix. Per-platform tuning is allowed; per-platform *inconsistency* is the killer.
